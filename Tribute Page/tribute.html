What I Admire About Jawaharlal Nehru


I deeply admire Jawaharlal Nehru's unwavering commitment to India's independence and his relentless pursuit of justice. His eloquence and leadership during the Indian independence movement were truly inspiring. Nehru's dedication to democracy, secularism, and social justice laid the foundation for a modern, diverse, and inclusive India that I admire today.



Biography of Jawaharlal Nehru


Jawaharlal Nehru, fondly known as "Pandit Nehru," was born on November 14, 1889, in Allahabad, India, into a distinguished family with a rich legacy of political involvement. His father, Motilal Nehru, was a prominent lawyer, and his mother, Swarup Rani Nehru, was deeply rooted in Indian traditions. After India gained independence on August 15, 1947, Jawaharlal Nehru assumed the role of the country's first Prime Minister, appointed by Lord Mountbatten, the last British Viceroy of India. Jawaharlal Nehru passed away on May 27, 1964, in New Delhi, leaving behind a legacy of leadership, democratic values, and a vision of a modern, secular, and industrialized India. His birthday, November 14, is celebrated as Children's Day in India, reflecting his deep affection for children and his commitment to their well-being.
